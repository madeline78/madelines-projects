North Carolina Flower and Fruit

Foliage found in North Carolina comes in many different colours, shapes, and sizes. Here are three of the many plants found in North Carolina that can be grown in your own backyard.

Wood-Anemone, Anemone quinquefolia

The wood-anemone flower is a small white flower that grows well in well-drained soil in forests. It is also known as the Thimbleweed. It's size generally only reaches one foot in height.

Carolina Lupine, Thermopsis villosa

The Carolina Lupine is another smaller flower but grows in groups off a main stem. It's pedals are a soft yellow colour, similar to daffodils. It grows well in full sun and well-drained soil to grow up to four feet tall.
